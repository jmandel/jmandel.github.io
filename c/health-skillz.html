03 / 08

The Core Difference: Who Can Build?

Direct Access (SMART on FHIR)

  • Anyone can build — solo developer, patient, hobbyist
  • No partnerships required
  • Data flows directly — no intermediary

I built Health Skillz in a weekend because the standards let me.

TEFCA Individual Access

To get your own data, you must become an "IAS Provider":

  • Contract with approved identity verification service
  • Privacy notices, consent collection, breach notification
  • Compliance obligations that survive 50 years

No "I just want my records" pathway.

04 / 08

What TEFCA Should Provide — But Doesn't

Three principles we advocated for (Mandel, Pollak, Mandl — JMIR 2022)

Principle Status
1. Patients can query for data about themselves PARTIAL
2. Patients can know when their data are queried and shared NOT YET
3. Patients can configure what is shared about them NOT YET

To be direct: I am critiquing TEFCA.

The networks have built policies and technical specifications that don't serve patient needs. Individual access still requires portal-by-portal login. There's no path for patients who want to build tools for themselves.

We deserve better.

07 / 08

What Data You Get — And Why Notes Matter

Structured (FHIR Resources)

What happened:

  • Condition — diagnosis codes
  • MedicationRequest — drug, dose, instructions
  • Observation — lab values, vitals
  • Procedure — what was done
  • Encounter — dates, providers

Unstructured (Clinical Notes)

Why it happened:

  • The clinician's reasoning
  • Differential diagnosis
  • Assessment and plan
  • What was discussed with the patient
  • Context that gives meaning to the codes
Structured: Metformin 500mg BID
Note: "Started for pre-diabetes given A1c trend and family history; patient preferred medication over waiting."
Structured: Creatinine 1.8 mg/dL (H)
Note: "Likely pre-renal from dehydration; baseline was normal; will recheck after fluids."

For AI to reason about your health, it needs the narrative — not just codes and values.

08 / 08

Key Takeaways

  1. Patient access APIs work today — legally mandated, no gatekeepers required
  2. Direct connection = full data — including clinical notes essential for AI reasoning
  3. Health Skillz: any AI agent can connect — open source, end-to-end encrypted, no intermediary
  4. Trade-off: You need to know where your data is and have portal logins
  5. TEFCA should do better — patients deserve visibility, control, and the ability to build their own tools
