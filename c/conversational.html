Four jobs with the same shape

  • Prior Authorization — eligibility for a therapy
  • Clinical Trial Matching — eligibility to enroll
  • Insurance Enrollment — eligibility for benefits
  • Specialist Referral — eligibility for a visit

What they have in common

  • Two sides: policy/requirements ↔ patient data
  • No one has the whole picture at the start
  • Conversation brings policy and data together in context

Across diverse jobs, dialogue reconciles policy needs and patient context.

2

Nudge vs. Sludge

✅ Keep the nudges

  • Evidence-aligned steps (e.g., conservative care first)
  • Clear paths to “yes”

⛔ Reduce the sludge

  • Hidden requirements, redundant forms
  • Late-stage surprises and delays

Transparency + conversation help keep constructive friction while minimizing arbitrary barriers.

5

Why APIs alone aren’t enough

What APIs do well

  • Structured, repeatable data exchange
  • Great when formats & workflows are pre-negotiated

Where APIs fall short

  • Real jobs vary — details aren’t all knowable upfront
  • Clinicians need earlier, upstream guidance

Da Vinci Burden Reduction (CRD/DTR/PAS): Centered around clipboard-style API that structures questions for providers to answer.

Useful infrastructure — but it frames the problem as “complete this form,” rather than enabling flexible conversations that align clinical decisions with policy before form-filling is even necessary.

6

Conversational interoperability

What it is

  • Agents negotiate what info is needed, in what form
  • Works even when rules and data start out incomplete

Two enablers

  • A2A: agent-to-agent dialogue
  • MCP: access to internal tools

Agents converse over A2A (two-way), while each side safely uses internal tools via MCP.

7

The road ahead: specific directions

Focus on jobs, not forms

  • Pilot agent-based interactions for prior auth and beyond
  • Surface policies & guidance earlier in workflows

Make it possible

  • Publish detailed policy criteria (Layer 2)
  • Adopt open protocols: A2A for dialogue, MCP for tools
  • Avoid over-specifying interop conversational steps; let agents adapt to variation

Outcome to aim for: right care, right patient, right time — with fewer late-stage surprises because guidance arrived upstream.
